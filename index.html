Space Missions

Apollo 11

First manned mission to land on the Moon, with astronauts Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin.

NASA July 20, 1969

Mars Perseverance

NASA rover searching for signs of ancient microbial life on Mars and collecting samples.

NASA February 18, 2021

Chandrayaan-3

Indian mission that successfully landed near the lunar south pole, making India the fourth country to achieve a soft landing on the Moon.

ISRO August 23, 2023

Voyager 1

The farthest human-made object from Earth, now in interstellar space providing data about the outer reaches of our solar system.

NASA September 5, 1977

Tiangong Space Station

China's space station module that hosts astronauts in low Earth orbit for scientific research.

CNSA April 29, 2021

ExoMars

European-Russian mission to search for evidence of life on Mars and study the Martian environment.

ESA/Roscosmos March 14, 2016

Aditya-L1

India's first mission to study the Sun. Positioned at the Lagrangian point L1, it observes solar activity and its effect on space weather.

ISRO September 2, 2023

James Webb Space Telescope

The largest, most powerful space telescope ever built, designed to observe the most distant objects in the universe.

NASA/ESA/CSA December 25, 2021

Mangalyaan (MOM)

India's first interplanetary mission and the first Asian mission to reach Mars orbit. Remarkable for its cost-effectiveness and first-attempt success.

ISRO September 24, 2014

Artemis Program

NASA's program to return humans to the Moon by the mid-2020s and establish sustainable lunar exploration by the end of the decade.

NASA & Partners 2020-Present

Gaganyaan

India's first human spaceflight program, aiming to send astronauts to low Earth orbit on an Indian launch vehicle developed by ISRO.

ISRO In Development

Hayabusa2

Japanese mission that returned samples from asteroid Ryugu, providing insights into the early solar system and potential origin of life.

JAXA December 6, 2020

Space Facts

The Sun accounts for 99.86% of the mass in our solar system

The Sun is so massive that it could fit roughly 1.3 million Earths inside it. Its immense gravity holds the entire solar system together.

There are more stars in the universe than grains of sand on Earth

Astronomers estimate there are about 100 billion stars in our Milky Way alone, and at least 100 billion galaxies in the observable universe.

A day on Venus is longer than its year

Venus rotates so slowly that it takes 243 Earth days to complete one rotation. However, it orbits the Sun in just 225 Earth days, making its day longer than its year.

Black holes aren't actually holes

They're extremely dense regions where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape. The term "black hole" was coined by physicist John Wheeler in 1967.

Jupiter's Great Red Spot is shrinking

This massive storm has been raging for at least 400 years, but recent observations show it's getting smaller. It's still large enough to fit about three Earths inside it.

India's Chandrayaan-1 discovered water on the Moon

In 2009, India's first lunar mission detected water molecules on the lunar surface, changing our understanding of the Moon. This discovery has significant implications for future lunar exploration and potential colonization.

The Apollo 11 computer had less processing power than a modern calculator

The guidance computer used for the Apollo 11 mission operated at 1.024 MHz and had about 64 kilobytes of memory, less powerful than many modern calculators or digital watches.

Space Pioneers

Neil Armstrong

American astronaut and the first person to walk on the Moon during the Apollo 11 mission in 1969. His famous words, "That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind," became one of the most iconic quotes in history.

Valentina Tereshkova

Soviet cosmonaut who became the first woman in space in 1963. She orbited the Earth 48 times aboard Vostok 6, spending almost three days in space and remains the only woman to have been on a solo space mission.

Kalpana Chawla

The first woman of Indian origin to go to space. She first flew on Space Shuttle Columbia in 1997 and tragically died during her second mission when Columbia disintegrated upon reentry in 2003.

Wernher von Braun

German-American aerospace engineer who was a leading figure in the development of rocket technology. He was the chief architect of NASA's Saturn V launch vehicle that propelled the Apollo spacecraft to the Moon.

Yuri Gagarin

Soviet pilot and cosmonaut who became the first human to journey into outer space. His Vostok spacecraft completed one orbit of Earth on April 12, 1961, marking a milestone in the Space Race.
